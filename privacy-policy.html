Privacy Policy

NAT Trade Privacy Policy

NAT Trade is a paper trading simulator. This policy explains what data is collected, why it is used, and how account data can be deleted.

Effective Date

June 8, 2026

Data Collected

NAT Trade may collect and store account and simulator data needed to operate the app.

  • Email address and username.
  • Mobile number provided during signup.
  • Watchlists and selected market symbols.
  • Paper trading history, active positions, pending orders, paper balance, and simulator state.
  • Chart drawings and indicator preferences.
  • Device session and login activity data for account security.

Email and Password Accounts

Users create NAT Trade accounts using email and password authentication. Passwords are handled by Supabase Auth and are not stored directly in NAT Trade application code.

Supabase Authentication

NAT Trade uses Supabase for authentication, email verification, password recovery, account sessions, and synchronization of user-owned app data. Supabase security controls and Row Level Security are used to help ensure users can access only their own data.

Watchlists

Watchlists are saved to your account so favorite market symbols can sync across mobile, web, and desktop app sessions.

Paper Trading History

NAT Trade stores simulated trade records, paper balance, active positions, pending orders, risk settings, and related history so users can review performance and restore their simulator state. NAT Trade does not execute real-money trades.

Local Storage Usage

The app may use browser local storage for device identifiers, remember-session preference, and local app state needed for a smooth user experience. Account-owned data is synchronized through Supabase when the user is logged in.

Account Deletion

Users can request deletion from within the Profile and Devices section. Account deletion removes the Supabase Auth account and associated NAT Trade data including watchlists, paper trading state, trade history, active sessions, and login activity.

Data Security

NAT Trade uses authenticated access and database-level security policies to protect user-owned data. Users are responsible for keeping their login credentials secure.
